Madrid

Welcome to Madrid

Discover Madrid, a city where history meets modern vibrancy. From the grand Royal Palace and the lively Plaza Mayor to world-class museums like the Prado, Madrid offers rich culture and stunning architecture.

Enjoy authentic Spanish flavors at Mercado de San Miguel, experience the city's legendary nightlife in Malasaña and Chueca, or relax in the serene Retiro Park.

Whether you're exploring landmarks, indulging in gastronomy, or immersing yourself in the local energy, Madrid promises an unforgettable experience.

  • Royal Palace of Madrid

    The Royal Palace of Madrid (Palacio Real de Madrid) is one of the most iconic landmarks in Spain and a must-visit destination for history and architecture lovers. As the official residence of the Spanish royal family, it stands as a grand symbol of Spain’s rich heritage and royal elegance.

    Built in the 18th century, the palace boasts an impressive Baroque and Neoclassical design, with over 3,400 rooms adorned with stunning frescoes, luxurious chandeliers, and exquisite tapestries. Visitors can explore the majestic Throne Room, the breathtaking Royal Armory, and the lavishly decorated Hall of Mirrors.

    Surrounded by the scenic Plaza de la Armería and the beautiful Sabatini Gardens, the palace offers breathtaking views and a peaceful atmosphere. Witness the grand Changing of the Guard ceremony or stroll through its historic halls to experience the grandeur of Spanish royalty.

    Whether you're a history enthusiast, an architecture admirer, or a traveler seeking cultural immersion, the Royal Palace of Madrid is an unforgettable highlight of the city.

  • Plaza Mayor

    The Plaza Mayor is one of Madrid’s most famous and historic squares, located in the heart of the city. Built in the 17th century under the reign of King Philip III, this grand plaza is a masterpiece of Spanish architecture and a central gathering place for locals and visitors alike.

    Surrounded by elegant three-story buildings with picturesque balconies and arcaded walkways, Plaza Mayor offers a unique atmosphere that blends history with modern-day vibrancy. At its center stands the iconic statue of Philip III, a symbol of Madrid’s royal past.

    Today, Plaza Mayor is a lively hub filled with traditional cafés, restaurants, and cultural events. Visitors can enjoy authentic Spanish flavors at Casa de la Panadería, explore artisan shops, or experience seasonal markets like the famous Christmas Market in December.

    Whether you're admiring the historic architecture, tasting Madrid’s delicious tapas, or simply soaking in the vibrant atmosphere, the Plaza Mayor is a must-visit landmark that captures the essence of the city.

  • Retiro Park

    Retiro Park (Parque del Buen Retiro) is a breathtaking green oasis in the heart of Madrid, offering a peaceful escape from the city's hustle and bustle. Covering over 125 hectares, this historic park is one of Madrid’s most beloved landmarks, blending natural beauty, artistic elegance, and cultural heritage.

    Originally a royal retreat in the 17th century, Retiro Park is now a public space filled with scenic pathways, lush gardens, and stunning monuments. The highlight of the park is the Great Pond (Estanque Grande), where visitors can enjoy a relaxing boat ride while admiring the majestic Monument to Alfonso XII.

    Other must-see attractions include the Crystal Palace (Palacio de Cristal), a breathtaking glass pavilion hosting contemporary art exhibitions, and the beautifully landscaped Rose Garden. Throughout the park, you’ll also find impressive fountains, open-air performances, and talented street artists.

    Whether you're looking to take a leisurely stroll, enjoy a picnic under ancient trees, or immerse yourself in Madrid’s rich history and culture, Retiro Park is a must-visit destination that offers something for everyone.
