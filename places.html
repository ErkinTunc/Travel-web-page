  • Madrid

    The capital of Spain, Madrid is a vibrant city where history and modernity come together. Renowned for its world-class museums like the Prado and Reina Sofia, Madrid is a cultural hub with masterpieces by artists such as Velázquez and Picasso. The city's historic neighborhoods, such as La Latina and Malasaña, are full of lively plazas, traditional tapas bars, and vibrant nightlife.

    Madrid is also famous for its beautiful parks like Retiro Park, where you can relax and enjoy the surroundings. The city's delicious cuisine, including jamón ibérico and cocido madrileño, adds to its charm. As Spain’s political and cultural center, Madrid offers a dynamic and welcoming atmosphere that makes it a must-visit destination.

  • Istanbul

    Turkey’s largest city, Istanbul straddles both Europe and Asia, blending rich history and culture. Known for iconic landmarks like the Hagia Sophia, Blue Mosque, and Topkapi Palace, Istanbul reflects its past as the capital of the Byzantine and Ottoman empires. The bustling Grand Bazaar and Spice Bazaar offer a taste of the city’s vibrant market culture.

    Istanbul’s neighborhoods, such as Beyoğlu and Karaköy, are filled with trendy cafes, galleries, and lively streets. The city’s food scene offers everything from street food like simit and kebabs to gourmet dining experiences. With stunning views along the Bosphorus and a unique blend of Eastern and Western influences, Istanbul is a city where the old and new come together beautifully.
